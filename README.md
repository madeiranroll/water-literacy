# 💧 Water Literacy

**An open-source, interactive guide to understanding water quality and finding the best water for your health.**

🌐 **[Live Demo →](https://water-literacy.vercel.app)**

---

## What it is

Water Literacy is a free, open-source web app that helps everyday people understand what's actually in their water — and get a personalised recommendation based on their health goals, location, and lifestyle.

Most people drink water every day without knowing their pH, TDS, mineral content, or what contaminants might be present. This tool bridges that gap with clear, science-backed education and a simple quiz that takes under two minutes.

Built with care by a health & environmental science professional who believes clean water knowledge should be accessible to everyone — not locked behind expensive consultants or confusing lab reports.

---

## Features

- **Water type guide** — Tap, filtered, spring, mineral, and purified water explained side by side with pros, cons, and best-use cases
- **Key parameters explained** — pH, TDS, key minerals (Ca, Mg, K), and contaminants to watch for
- **Personalised quiz** — 5 questions covering your health goals, location, water source, concerns, and health considerations
- **Smart recommendations** — Logic-driven results covering what to look for, what to avoid, and one actionable pro tip
- **Zero dependencies** — Pure HTML, CSS and JavaScript. No frameworks, no tracking, no ads
- **Dark mode ready** — Fully adapts to system preferences
- **Accessible** — Semantic HTML, screen-reader friendly, keyboard navigable

---

## Who it's for

- Anyone curious about improving their hydration and health
- People in areas with water quality concerns (old pipes, rural wells, industrial zones)
- Athletes optimising electrolyte intake
- Pregnant or immunocompromised individuals needing safer water guidance
- Environmental educators and health coaches

## Getting started

No installation needed. Just open `index.html` in any browser.

```bash
git clone https://github.com/madeiranroll/water-literacy.git
cd water-literacy
open index.html
```

Or deploy instantly to Vercel / Netlify by connecting this repo — it just works.

## The science behind it

The recommendations in this app are based on:

- **WHO Guidelines for Drinking-water Quality** (4th edition)
- **EPA National Primary & Secondary Drinking Water Regulations**
- **EWG Tap Water Database** methodology
- **NSF International** filter certification standards

This tool is educational and does not replace professional medical or environmental advice. If you have a serious health condition, consult a qualified professional.
